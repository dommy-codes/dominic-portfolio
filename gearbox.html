Gearbox

Working on the reduction gearbox was insightful to say the least. I gained practical experience in the shop, extending my knowledge on mechanical systems and the effect of parameters and goals on design philosphy. Created several calculation models, experiment with ratio combinations between pinions and gears, and shaft splines. Compared to the previous year, the reduction ratio was increased from 5.3 to 6.7. The purpose of the gearbox was to increase the baseline torque of our Kohler engine which only outputted around 10 hp. The actual transmission was done using a belt driven CVT (Countinuous Variable Transmission).

Some of the manfacturing methods used included manual lathing for the shafts, CNC milling for the housing, and grinding down gear blanks to fit the tight tolerance requirements.
